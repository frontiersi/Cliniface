Overview

Cliniface gives clinicians the means to interactively visualise, measure, analyse, and export data about 3D facial images taken of their patients. A comprehensive range of facial measurements are taken and compared against statistics of normal facial growth to automatically identify notable phenotypic traits as standard Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) terms having disease / gene associations. By combining these HPO terms with other phenotypic information (including medical / family histories), clinicians are better able to quickly and accurately arrive at a potential diagnosis for their patients. The results can be summarised in a PDF report, or exported to CSV or other text formats for detailed follow-up investigations.

Cliniface takes over fifty measurements from different parts of the face including distances, depth, angles, and asymmetry. Users can also record their own measurements on the facial surface. For treatment monitoring and other kinds of shape analysis, a second image can be loaded and all measurements directly compared between the two. Visualisations of asymmetry, difference, and curvature are also provided to more effectively interpret the facial surface.

Cliniface can work with 3D images stored in a wide variety of formats including those without texture information. Basic tools to help fix aspects of the model's geometry are built-in for convenience. All processing and analysis is carried out locally on your own machine and no data are remotely uploaded anywhere thereby ensuring the protection and privacy of yours and your patient's data.

Cliniface is free and open source. Please help us to improve Cliniface and let us know if you'd like to use it as part of your own research.
